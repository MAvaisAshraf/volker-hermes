Hidden Portraits

Polyhedric Portraits

Ten years in the making, Hidden Portraits reinterprets historical paintings through a modern lens.

Using only elements from the original work, German artist Volker Hermes visually manipulates famous Old Masters portraits, exploring expression when the face is obscured. By blocking the emotions expressed by the face, Hermes invites viewers to observe ancient codes of fashion as symbols of self-representation and social status. Then, with a contemporary perspective, great respect, and a dash of humor, Hermes layers the codes of our times over the codes of ancient times.

“Self-representation has always been a fertile creative territory for artists, portraiture being a visual medium that has most directly expressed concepts of identity, emotional individuality, gender, and social status—issues around which today’s conversations gravitate and which are also obliquely yet imaginatively addressed in the work of the German painter Volker Hermes.” - Vogue
